# Market-Evidence Survey — Brazil / LatAm Clinical Trials

*Run **during the hackathon** by a Claude Code session connected via **MCP to Prolific**:
Claude helped design the instrument, recruit a qualified pool, and analyze the results.
This is both a market-validation artifact (Impact) and an example of Claude-as-analyst
(Claude Use). Aggregate results only — no individual responses are published.*

**Fieldwork:** July 10–11, 2026 · **N = 50** complete responses · recruited via Prolific.
**Qualified ICP (pharma / biotech / CRO / clinical-adjacent):** 31 of 50 (62%).
**Respondent HQ:** UK/EU 48% · US 38% · Other 12% · Brazil 2%.

---

## Headline findings

1. **The awareness gap is the story.** **70%** had never heard of **Lei 14.874/2024** (single
   ethics review ~30 business days + ANVISA capped at 90 business days). Only 6% knew the details.
2. **One paragraph of information moves intent.** After reading a one-line description of the new
   law, likelihood to include Brazil in the next 12 months rose from **3.44 → 4.54 mean (+1.10)**,
   paired *t* ≈ 4.35, **p < .001**. 50% raised their score, 44% held, only 6% dropped.
3. **The effect is strongest among buyers.** Qualified ICP subset (n=31): **3.77 → 5.16 (+1.39)**;
   high-intent (7–10) respondents nearly doubled (23% → 39%).
4. **The #1 barrier is exactly what the law fixed** — regulatory approval timelines (36% overall,
   42% ICP; also the top single dealbreaker at 20%). The market is pricing Brazil on outdated
   information.
5. **They want de-risking, not discounts:** vetted quality-certified site network (42%), local
   regulatory/feasibility experts (40%), proof via recent fast start-up case studies (30%).
6. **The window to influence is early:** 60% lock their country list at concept or feasibility
   stage — before a CRO is even selected (8%).

## Why this matters for TrialBridge

The survey validates the core thesis with primary data: the barrier to Brazil is **information
and provable de-risking**, not fundamentals — and buyers decide **early**, at the feasibility
stage, which is exactly where TrialBridge operates. The product's sponsor side supplies the
provable numbers (patients, sites, infrastructure); its site side supplies the fast, structured
feasibility answers buyers say they want.

## Method notes

- Instrument: pre/post natural-experiment design (measure intent, expose one factual paragraph
  about Lei 14.874/2024, re-measure) — isolates the effect of the information itself.
- Significance computed on the paired within-respondent change (df = 49).
- Full item-by-item breakdown is retained privately with the analysis; only aggregates appear here.
